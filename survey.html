The studies of the Research Department “Astronomical Surveys” relate to discovery and investigation of new objects based on Byurakan First (FBS – Markarian) and Second (SBS) surveys, as well as to comparative analysis of all-sky and large area multiwavelength astronomical surveys, and revelation of new interesting objects by means of cross-correlations of these surveys. The main thematic is: search, studies and statistics of active galaxies. The studied objects are: blazars, quasars, Seyfert galaxies, LINERs, Ultra-Luminous InfraRed Galaxies (ULIRGs) and Starburst (SB) galaxies. Multiwavelength catalogues and databases are widely used: ROSAT, GALEX, SDSS, 2MASS, WISE, IRAS, AKARI, NVSS, FIRST and others. The “extragalactic” researchers of the Department are Areg Mickaelian (studies of Markarian galaxies, statistics of active galaxies, blazars, quasars, Seyfert galaxies, ULIRGs), Marietta Gyulzadyan (studies of Markarian and SBS galaxies), Hayk Abrahamyan (radio galaxies, blazars, quasars, and radio radiation of active galaxies), Gurgen Paronyan (X-ray galaxies, blazars, quasars and X-ray radiation of active galaxies), Gor Mikayelyan (IR galaxies and IR radiation of active galaxies). The “galactic” researchers of the Department are Kamo Gigoyan and Gayane Kostandyan. Their works relate to studies of late-type (M, C) stars using multiwavelength databases: ROSAT, SDSS, 2MASS, WISE, IRAS, AKARI, NSVS, Catalina, LINEAR and others.
